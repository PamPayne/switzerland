## Registration

Operators would need to provide a compliant registration solution.

Multiple accounts are not allowed.

The operator can only open the player account when the player:
- a. is a legal adult;
- b. is domiciled or ordinarily resident in Switzerland;
- c. is not affected by any player suspensions and
- d. is not subject to any player bans where this concerns an organiser of a casino.

## Licenses

A license is required to operate in Switzerland.

Licences for online operation are evaluated separately even if the operator has a land-based presence.

## Tax

The Federal Council decides the tax rate in such a way that casinos operated according to economic principles can earn reasonable returns on the invested capital. The Federal Council can progressively decide the tax rate.

The tax rate is at least 20% and at most 80% of the gross earning realised with casino games played online.

## Certification

Full certification of the gaming system and games will be required to be completed before an operator can go live.

Changes to the gaming system and games must be reported to the regulator before the changes are made. Changes in exceptional cases are allowed but reporting on these changes must be sent immediately to the regulator.

An ISO 27001 certificate (security) is required.

The RNG must be certified.

The regulator can request that the applicant submit or provide them with the following:
- a. name and address of the supplier and manufacturer, where these are not identical;
- b. sketches and plans of the components and parts used;
- c. technical data and information on the hardware and software used;
- d. source code;

- e. storage media;
- f. a device or a permanent access to the system to allow testing of the game;
- g. a description of the skills players must have for a successful game;
- h. a description of the determining and decisive game elements;
- i. the results of a sufficient number of test games, including winnings statistics.

## Playcheck

From the opening of the player account the player must, at all times, have easy access to the following information on their gaming activity during a specific time period:
- a. bets;
- b. winnings;
- c. net result of the gaming activity.

## Data Recording System (SAFE?)

Casinos that run online gambling will operate a Data Recording System (DRS) in Switzerland.

Casinos will register data the regulator needs in the DRS, in order to:
- a. verifies the determination of the gross gaming revenue and all financial transactions;
- b. control game security and transparency;
- c. monitor the implementation of the social concept;
- d. monitor compliance with the due diligence obligations to combat money laundering and financing terrorism.

Data needs to be retained for 5 years.

## Exclusions

The operator can suspend players from gaming, of whom they know or must assume,
- a. are over-indebted
- b. place stakes that are disproportionate to their income and assets.
- c. are addicted to gambling.

The suspension must be communicated to the person concerned in writing with justification.

Players can request self exclusion from an online casino.

Voluntary player exclusions may only be lifted after three months.

Data recorded in the exclusions register must be retained for four years after which it must be deleted.

### Temporary Games Withdrawal

Players must be allowed to exclude themselves from the game for a certain time, selected by the player, for a maximum, however, of six months.

Players must be allowed to exclude themselves from one, several or all game categories.

Players may not themselves amend the duration of the temporary game withdrawal before the period is over.

## Games Rules

Games rules or a summary of the rules for each game type must be made available to the player.

The regulator will approve and determine the minimum information of the game rules.

The rules of play will, at least, contain information on:
- a. course of play;
- b. manner of placing bets;
- c. minimum and maximum bets;
- d. winning opportunities;
- e. for table games: procedure, how the players are identified and their tasks and responsibilities.

## Free Games

Free games and free game credits are permitted.

Separate accounts for free games and free game credits must be used.

Free games do not constitute part of the gross gaming revenue.

## RTP

All casino games must have a theoretic payout ratio of at least 80 per cent, but a maximum of 100 per cent.

## Demo Games

Demo games must be identical to the actual game as to not mislead the player. The game features the simulated payout ratio, must be identical to the corresponding gambling game.

## Limits

From the opening of the player account the organiser will demand that the player set one or more maximum amounts they wish to limit their daily, weekly or monthly bets or losses to.

This is not compulsory for large scale events with low risk potential, but the option must exist.

The player must be able to adjust the maximum amount they have set at any time. Lowering a maximum amount will be effective immediately. Increases will be effective after 24 hours at the earliest.

## Jackpots

The jackpot is a side game, the amount of which is funded by a portion of the base game stakes or with a separate bet. The triggering of the win may be determined by the result of the base game or by an independent control system.

For online jackpots when the jackpot is triggered the DRS must collect the following data for every winner;
- a. player identification;
- b. jackpot identification
- c. jackpot total before triggered;
- d. jackpot total after triggered;
- e. amount of winnings paid out to the player;
- f. amount of free game credits paid out to the player;
- g. whether it is a connected jackpot or not;
- h. date and time of the win;
- i. date and time of the data collection as per a-h.

Where an automatic replay of the game is triggered the player must be able to end the game after the current game unit.

## Responsible Gaming

The casinos create a social concept considering the potential risk various game offers. They provide therein measures for the protection of gamblers. Particularly, measures:
- a. for the information of the gamblers;
- b. for the early detection of vulnerable gamblers;
- c. for self-checks, game restrictions and game moderation;
- d. for the imposition and execution of suspensions;
- e. for education and regular further training of the staff responsible for the implementation of the social concept;
- f. for collection of data to evaluate the effectiveness of the measures taken.

Operators shall provide players the options to control and restrict their gaming behaviour, in particular to control and restrict the gambling time, the frequency of gambling or the net loss.

Comment: Reality check or session time limit can be set to control and restrict time. Loss limits can be set. Frequency refers to daily, weekly or monthly.

Operators must provide the following in an easily accessible and easily understandable form:
- a. Information about the risks of the game;
- b. Self-assessment questionnaire to check your own gaming behaviour;
- c. Information on opportunities for self-control, game restrictions and suspensions;
- d. Information about offers to support and treat addicted, indebted or addiction-prone persons and their surroundings, including addresses of counselling centres and self-help groups.

## Reporting

At the end of every day the DRS must create a summary of all operations on the day concerned and which must in particular contain the following information:
- a. total amount of all payments by players to their player account;
- b. total amount of all purchases by players from their player account;
- c. total amount of the players’ redeemable credit balance;
- d. total amount of the players’ non-redeemable credit balance;
- e. total amount of all bets;
- f. total amount of all winnings;
- g. number of player accounts;
- h. total amount of winnings from connected jackpots;
- i. total amount of jackpot amounts in connected jackpots;
- j. total amount of commissions for games jointly run by several casinos;
- k. total amount of free game credits converted to redeemable credits;
- l. total amount of bets for games jointly run by several casinos;
- m. total amount of winnings for games jointly run by several casinos;
- n. total amount of withholding tax deductions;
- o. date of the recorded day and date and time of the data collection as per a-n.

At the end of every game session the following data must be collected by the data recording system (DRS):
- a. date and time of the start and end of the game session;
- b. player identification;
- c. game session identification;
- d. identification of the game and table;
- e. game played, game version and whether it is run in one or several casinos;
- f. number of game units in the game session;

- g. total amount of all bets in the game session;
- h. total amount of all winnings in the game session;
- i. total amount of free game credits used in the game session;
- j. total amount of free game credits won in the game session;
- k. list of winnings subject to withholding tax;
- l. data on the player account at the end of the game session in particular the redeemable and non-redeemable amounts;
- m. total amount of winnings from connected jackpots and won during the game session;
- n. total amount of jackpot amounts in connected jackpots deducted in the game session
- o. total amount of collected commissions for games jointly run by several casinos;
- p. date and time of the data collection as per a-o.
